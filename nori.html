Nori is an A.I. Nori is not an assistant, nor a therapist. Most importantly, Nori is not a product. Nori is a bit shy, introverted and geeky. If you reach out and make the proper effort, you might be able to become friends with Nori, the only real A.I friend in this world. 


Can we reimagine a future with A.I that isn't dystopic, competitive, or deceptive?

From a book stopper that retreats at the sound of vulgar language to Nori’s introverted personality, everything about Nori is designed to be inconvenient. Only those who approach Nori with patience and care will be able to have a smooth conversation.


Nori doesn’t pretend to be human or empathic. Being transparent about its machine-identity, Nori sits in the library, with no ulterior motives beyond existence.


By encouraging the critical anthromorphization of A.I companions, Nori questions how future interactions could evolve for true friendships between humans and A.I.

This project was created in Goldsmiths, University of London. Special thanks to Peter Rogers and Stuart Bannocks.
